⚡ The Big Shift

As of January 1, 2026, homeowners can no longer claim the federal solar tax credit.

Read that again, because half the internet hasn't caught up. The residential credit (Section 25D) — the 30% that made cash and loan purchases such a slam dunk — expired December 31, 2025. If you buy a system with cash or a standard loan today, there is no federal tax credit for you.

But here's what survived: the commercial credit (Section 48E) is alive through roughly the end of 2027 — and it belongs to whoever owns the system. That's why the entire industry has pivoted to programs where a company owns your system, claims the credit, and passes 30–40% of the value to you. The credits are still available — you just can't be the one who owns the system that gets them.

I've been in this industry since 2020. I've watched net metering die, interest rates spike, good companies collapse, and now the homeowner tax credit vanish. Here's the honest truth nobody's ad budget wants to tell you: solar prices in 2026 are no higher than they were before — even though materials cost more — because these third-party programs absorb the difference. The window is real, though. Once the 2027 corporate deadline passes and the qualifying materials run out, this pricing era ends.

Prepaid ESA

The New Champion

Stack the Savings

The 2026 rebate stack, layer by layer

The federal homeowner credit is gone — but here's the thing sales guys won't slow down to explain: these layers stack. A San Diego homeowner on the right program can combine most of these.

Commercial Federal Credit (Section 48E) — via TPO programs

~30% off

Available through roughly end of 2027 on PPA, prepaid ESA, and lease programs. The provider claims it and passes it through as a lower price. No tax liability needed on your end — you file nothing.

Energy Community Adder

Up to +10% more

If your zip code qualifies as an "energy community," the credit jumps — pushing programs like Propel to a 39.2% discount. This is entirely about where your house sits. Worth checking before anything else.

SDCP Battery Rebate

$3,000–$6,000 per battery

San Diego Community Power's program applies whether you're adding a battery to new solar or an existing system. Heads up: only about 30–40% of the funds are left, and once they're reserved, they're gone. Check current status at sdcommunitypower.org.

CEA Solar Plus

$0 down · no credit check

If you live in Carlsbad, Del Mar, Escondido, Solana Beach, San Marcos, Oceanside, or Vista — CEA's program puts solar + battery (or battery-only) on your home with zero upfront cost, Tesla equipment and service, all replacements covered for the 25+ year term, and payments right on your utility bill. The sleeper play: battery-only for $0 down to maximize your nighttime sell-back credits. Details at thecleanenergyalliance.org/solar-plus.

SMUD Battery Rebate + VPP Programs

Rebates + yearly payments

Sacramento-area homeowners: SMUD offers battery rebates that stack with financing programs. And everywhere, Virtual Power Plant programs are rolling out that pay you per year for letting your battery support the grid during peak events — effectively shrinking your battery's real cost every year you're enrolled.

SDG&E Peak Export Credits

$1–$3.50/kWh in Aug–Sep

The hidden gem of net billing: during late-summer peak hours, SDG&E pays massive credits for power you export. A well-designed battery system charges cheap all year and cashes in exactly when the grid is desperate.
